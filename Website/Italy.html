The Colosseum is a gigantic, stone amphitheater - the largest built in Rome. It could fit 50000 people to watch extreme exhibits, such as gladiator fights or executions. It is currently Rome's most popular attraction alongside the Vatican City. In Rome, the Colosseum is located on the east bankof the River Tiber, a historical river relating to the origin of Rome. For construction, a flat area was chosen in between three hills (the Caelian, Esquiline and Palatine). The Colosseum was built in 70 AD. The Flavian Dynasty that started in 69 AD only lasted about 25 years, but many advancements were made, including the Colosseum's construction.
