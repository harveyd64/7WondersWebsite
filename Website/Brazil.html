The Christ the Redeemer statue is a 30 metre tall Art Deco rendition of Jesus Christ. The statue is made of reinforced concrete clad, and sits on an 8 metre high pedestal. The 28 metre arm span is also massive: it is the largest Art Deco statue in the world. Based in Rio de Janeiro, the statue has had massive influence over the area. House prices often increase depending on the view of the statue - even slight views. It actually wasn't built in Brazil, but in France at first, before being shipped. The foundation stone of the statue was laid on 04.04.1922 - the day Brazil was granted independence from Portugal. Construction finished in 1931 and the reinforced concrete was organised in a mosaic of triangle tiles. It honors Princess Isabel of Brazil.
