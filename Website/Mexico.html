The Chichén Itzá is a set of Mayan ruins. During pre-Columbian times it was a highly populated, diverse city with massive economic and political power. As an active archeological site, more and more discoveries are being located every day. The city was on the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico. It's over 100 miles away from Cancun, a modern resort in Mexico. The name translates to "the mouth at the well of Itza" which might refer to the 4 natural cenotes with their plentiful water supplies. The build period is unclear - however some accounts record its build date among the early 400s (AD). It is clear that the site became an economic powerhouse by the 600s (AD) - it was one of the largest Mayan cities at this point.
