The City of Petra, which was once a massive trade centre, is now in ruins. The mountains surrounding Petra acted as a strong protector - the word Petra is Greek for "STONE", representing its carving into the mountains. Located in Jordon, the City of Petra isn't too far away from Israel's border. It's about 150 miles from Jerusalem. The sandstone colour has led to the city being known as "Rose City" as well as the surrounding desert area. A tribe known as the Nabataeans built Petra in 312 BC - the building depicts their incredible skill at a variety of architectural styles. The city was abandoned shortly before the 8th Century and wasn't found again until 1812 by an explorer named Johann Ludwig Burckhardt.
